# 适合读大学的城市候选池（2026）

Use this file when the user asks about university cities, city choice, internships, industry opportunities, or school/major/city tradeoffs.

## Core Logic

City choice matters because internships, local industry,校招资源,生活成本,交通,气候,饮食, and future settlement opportunities shape four years of development. But city is not always more important than school or major:

- High score/top platform: usually school layer first, then major, then city.
- Middle/low score: major and employment path often beat city glamour.
- Medicine: region has unusually high weight because employment is city/region-bound.
- Humanities, finance, media, law: city and school network can strongly affect internship and early career.
- Engineering: choose cities with matching industries, not just "big city" labels.

Always combine city with the student's score/rank, target major, family finances, and willingness to leave home.

## How To Use The City List

1. Match industry first. Example: chip/IC can prioritize Shanghai, Nanjing, Shenzhen, Xi'an, Hefei; medical tracks can look at Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Harbin, Chongqing.
2. Match school layer second. A weaker school in a hot city may not beat a stronger school with better升学/行业口碑.
3. Check personal fit: climate, diet, distance from home, cost, language/culture, and ability to stay after graduation.
4. For city-driven choices, verify actual campus location. Many universities have multiple campuses, and the target major may not be in the city center.
5. Do not overfit rankings. Treat this as a候选池, not a universal ranking for every student.

## City Question Answer Template

When answering, use this structure:

1. "先看专业和就业方向，再看城市。"
2. Shortlist 3-5 cities by industry and score range.
3. Name representative schools only as leads.
4. Add checks: campus location, internships, household cost, climate/diet, admissions plan.
5. Close with decision ownership: city can open doors, but the final choice must fit the student's constraints.
